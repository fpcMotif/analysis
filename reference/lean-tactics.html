Lean Tactics — Quick Reference

A living cheat sheet for formalizing Tao's Analysis. Tactics marked L1/L2 you have met (by lesson); the rest are previews you'll meet soon.

Closing a goal

rfl L1 Closes a = b when the two sides are definitionally equal — equal after Lean computes/unfolds definitions (e.g. 2 + 2 = 4).
exact h soon Closes the goal when h is exactly a proof of it. The Lean version of "this is precisely our hypothesis."
assumption soon Closes the goal if some hypothesis already in context matches it, without you naming which.
decide soon Closes a decidable goal by computation (small finite checks, concrete numeric (in)equalities).

Rewriting & computing

rw [h] L1 Given h : x = y, replaces every x in the goal with y (left → right). Use rw [← h] to rewrite right → left. The workhorse: most early proofs are chains of rewrites.
simp soon Simplifies the goal using a large library of marked lemmas. Powerful, but can feel like a black box — use deliberately.
ring soon Proves equalities that hold in any commutative ring — the algebraic identities you'd "just expand" on paper.
norm_num soon Settles concrete numeric goals and (in)equalities over ℕ, ℤ, ℚ, ℝ.
linarith soon Closes goals that follow by linear arithmetic from the hypotheses — the bread and butter of ε–δ estimates.
omega soon Decision procedure for linear arithmetic over ℤ and ℕ.

Structuring a proof

intro h L2 Moves the antecedent of an implication (or a ∀-bound variable) into the context — "assume h; let x be arbitrary." In the induction step, intro n ih names the predecessor and the hypothesis.
apply f L2 Reduces the goal to the hypotheses of f — backward reasoning, "it suffices to show…". The companion inducts via apply induction.
induction n L2 Splits a goal about n : ℕ into a base case and a successor case (with the induction hypothesis in context) — Tao's Axiom 2.5 made mechanical. Two syntaxes: companion revert n; apply induction then . <base> and intro n ih; mainstream induction n with | zero => … | succ k ih => ….
revert n L2 Moves a local variable back into the goal as a ∀ — exposes the quantifier for apply induction, and generalizes so the induction hypothesis stays strong enough. Inverse of intro.
obtain soon Destructs an existential or conjunction: obtain ⟨x, hx⟩ := h names the witness and its property.
constructor soon Splits a goal built from "and" / "iff" into its parts to prove separately.
calc soon Writes a chain of (in)equalities with a justification on each step — the closest Lean gets to a textbook computation.

The escape hatch

sorry L1 "Assume this goal holds." Lets a file compile with gaps. Every exercise in the companion is a sorry you will replace.
