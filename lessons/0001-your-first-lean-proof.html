Lesson 1 · The tactic state

Your first Lean proof

You can already prove things about ℕ on paper. This lesson is about a new medium for the same act — and the one mental shift that unlocks all of Lean.

In Tao's Chapter 2 you proved facts about the natural numbers from the Peano axioms — that 0 + n = n, that addition is commutative, all of it. You know why those proofs work. The only thing you don't yet know is how to say them to a machine so it checks every step. That is the entire skill we are building, and it starts with one idea.

The one idea. A Lean proof is not a paragraph you write and submit. It is a goal you transform, one tactic at a time, until nothing is left to prove. Lean always shows you the current tactic state; your job is to read it and pick a tactic that moves it forward.1

That is the shift. On paper, the proof is the artifact. In Lean, the proof is a dialogue: you act, Lean shows you the new state, you act again. When the state reads “No goals”, you're done.

Two tactics are enough to start

You only need two verbs to make your first real proofs about ℕ — the same two that carry you through the opening of the game below.

rfl — “these are the same by definition.” It closes a goal a = b when the two sides become identical once Lean unfolds definitions and computes. For instance 2 + 2 = 4 closes by rfl, because addition on ℕ is defined recursively and Lean just runs it.2

rw [h] — “rewrite using this equation.” Given a hypothesis or lemma h : x = y, it replaces every x in the goal with y, left to right. This is exactly what you do on paper when you say “by the definition of addition, succ(n) + m = succ(n + m), so the goal becomes…”.

Watch the state evolve. Suppose addition satisfies add_succ : succ n + m = succ (n + m) and we want to rewrite the left side of a goal:

Tactic state — before
n m : ℕ  ⊢  succ n + m = succ (n + m)
▼  rw [add_succ]
Tactic state — after
n m : ℕ  ⊢  succ (n + m) = succ (n + m)
▼  rfl
Tactic state — after
No goals

That is a complete Lean proof: rw [add_succ] then rfl. Notice you never “wrote a paragraph” — you steered the state to No goals.

Now do it for real (≈20 min)

Quizzes build the model; a real checker builds the skill. The Natural Number Game is the perfect place — it builds ℕ from the Peano axioms you already know from Tao Chapter 2, runs in the browser with nothing to install, and checks every tactic instantly.

  1. Open the game (link below) and start Tutorial World — 8 short levels.
  2. You'll meet rfl first, then rw. After every tactic, look at the goal panel and say to yourself what changed.
  3. Tutorial World ends by proving 2 + 2 = 4. When the panel says “Level completed! 🎉”, you've closed your first real goals.
  4. If you have momentum, start Addition World — it introduces induction, which is the bridge to everything else, and is Lesson 2.
